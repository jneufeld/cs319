Project Risks

  1. We don't get an accurate statement of requirements
  2. The client doesn't reply
  3. Not all team members are able to attend a meeting due to coop/work interviews

We don't get an accurate statement of requirements

Description: After meeting with the client we are still unsure or unaware of many of the project requirements. We don't have a good idea or solid foundation to work on.

Probability: 3 (may happen)

Impact: 4 (serious impact)

Mitigation: Plan for the meeting with the client to ensure we get the information we need. Put time into coming up with what we need to ask and how we need to ask it.

Contingency: Schedule our second client meeting as soon as possible, and learn from our mistakes with the first meeting so that we thoroughly prepare for it.

The client doesn't reply

Description: The client may not respond to our initial email for a number of days, putting us behind with respect to time.

Probability: 3 (may happen)

Impact: 1 (little impact)

Mitigation: Reply to the client as soon as possible when we're able.

Contingency: If the client has not responded, send a new email or a gentle reminder.

Not all team members are able to attend a meeting due to coop/work interviews

Description: As some team members are looking for their summer term co-op jobs, they will be required to go on interviews on a short notice. These interviews may cause them to miss team meetings which may cause some communication issues.

Probability: 3 (may happen)

Impact:

Mitigation: Find multiple meeting times for the team during the week. If no other time works, the member who will not be attending the meeting must send out a full review of their work and what they must say; as the agenda will be sent out prior to the meeting, the member must also comment on the agenda items to make sure their opinion is heard.
